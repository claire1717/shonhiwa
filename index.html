Creating Awareness on Child Marriages

Empowering Children for a Brighter Future

Introduction

Child marriages refers to any formal or informal union regardless if one or both parties are under the age of 18 . It is the violation of human rights and particularly affect girls depriving them of their childhood and leads to school dropouts therefore , resulting in negative consequences such as early pregnancy ,maternal health risks , social isolation and increased vulnerability to domestic violence .


In Zimbabwe, child marriage remains a significant issue, particularly affecting girls.More than 20% of girls aged 15 to 18 years and even below are currently in a union.This statistics clearly shows a sharp rural-urban divide , with two in five women in rural areas getting married before 18 years , compared to one to five in urban areas like Harare. Specially, in Harare, 21.7% of girls are married bt the age of 18, reflecting the persistent challenge of child marriage in urban settings

Causes

      Poverty:Families in Highfields may marry off their daughters at a young age due to economic hardships ,seeing it as a way to reduce financial burden on the family .
      Tradition and cultural norms:Deeply cultural practices and traditions may dictate that girls should marry young , perpetuating the cycle of child marriage.
      Limited access to education: Many barriers to education for example lack of schools and resources therefore ,some family seeing marriage the best alternative .
      Lack of legal enforcement :Weak enforcement of laws and legal protections against child marriages may allow the practice to persist .

Effects

      Health risks :Child brides in face increased risks of complications during pregnancy
      Interrupted education : marriage often results in girls dropping out of schools , limiting their opportunities for personal and economic development .
      Psychological impact :Child brides may experience mental health issues such as depression , anxiety , and low self-esteem due to lack of autonomy and agency in their marriage .
      Inter-generational cycle :Girls who marry early are more likely to have children at a young age , perpetuating the cycle of poverty and early marriage for next generation .
      Domestic violence : brides in are at a higher risk or experiencing physical , emotional , and sexual violence from their spouses.
      Poverty : brides may lack the education and skills to secure stable employment ,perpetuating the cycle of poverty.
      Gender inequality: marriages reinforces gender disparities by limiting girls autonomy , agency , and opportunities for empowerment.

Purposes

Raising Awareness
Educating the comunity about the negative impacts of child marriage on health , education , and economic prospects
Empower Girls
Provide resources and support to young girls to pursue education and delay marriage .
Community Engagement
Foster dialogue within the community to challenge and change cultural norms that pepertuate child marriage ..

Goals

Reduce Child Marriage
Aim to reduce the incidence of child marriage in Highfield by at least 50% over the next five years.
Support Services
Establish support services for at-risk girls, including counseling,legal aid, and educational scholarship
Policy Advocacy
Work with local authorities to strengthen and enforce laws against child marriages
